2. Principles and Boundaries

A Principles
The framework has been developed using the following principles:
(a) Description not prescription (the “permissive” part of the CRAG proposal): Avoid arbitrary rules and value judgements, while also recognising that certain boundaries are required if method ringing is to maintain its current form and structure.
(b) Simple, generic and consistent (the “simple” part of the CRAG proposal): The more straightforward the new framework is, the more widely it will be understood across the ringing community and the more accessible it will be to new ringers joining the Exercise. At the same time, it is recognised that method ringing has some inherent complexity resulting from both its mathematical foundation and its rich history, such that not all complexity can be eliminated.
(c) Define, explain and communicate: Terms used in the framework should be clearly defined. A supporting document to the new framework should also be developed to fully explain the new framework to the ringing community. A forum should also be established to answer questions on the new framework.
(d) Continuity: Most standard methods and performances should continue to be described as they are today, or with only minimal alterations in the interests of standardisation and simplification. The greater permissiveness of the new framework will mostly be of interest to more advanced ringers who wish to explore new forms of method ringing.
B Boundaries
For the purposes of the new framework, the following boundaries were used in defining the scope of method ringing:
(a) Rows In method ringing, the same set of bells rings in every row, with each bell ringing exactly once in every row.
(b) Places In method ringing, each bell rings in a different place in the row to every other bell.
(e) Changes

The definition of Change has been expanded to include any transformation of one row into the next. This facilitates the definition of jump changes and identity changes. The term adjacent change has been introduced to be equivalent to the previous definition of Change (in Decision E.(A).1).

(c) Truth

Truth is based upon the n+1 principle i.e. 0 to n complete extents + 1 partial extent. In other words a block of rows is considered true if either all its rows are distinct (only occur once) or if the block is longer than an extent, the remaining rows are distinct after removing any complete extents, regardless of where these rows occur in the block.

(d) Cover Bells

Rows may have more than one cover bell. Fixed cover places are excluded when determining truth, while variable cover places are included. “Fixed cover place”, “variable cover place” and “true” are all defined in the new framework.

(e) Length and Stage

Ringing of all lengths on all stages should be treated alike for simplicity, consistency and permissiveness. The definitions should be relevant and applicable to all ringing, not just peals. Hence the definition of true applies equally to touches, quarter peals and peals, and if 5000 changes is a peal of Major, then it is also sufficient for a peal of Minor.

(f) Peal Ringing

The Conditions for Peal Ringing in Decision D have been replaced by performance reporting requirements. Under this new permissive framework, the onus is placed upon the bands ringing a performance to determine if a performance merits publication. The requirement is to report any non-standard characteristic in the performance report such that this can be used in subsequent analyses.
