2.

In-course course-heads are those courses that can be reached with bobs only (14 or 1[n-2]) when starting from rounds. Out-of-course course-heads are thoses courses that can only be reached with bobs plus an odd number of singles (1234 or 1[n-2][n-1][n]) when starting from rounds.

False course-head groups designated by uppercase letters (A to U) contain in-course false course-heads, and may also contain out-of-course false course-heads. Groups designated by lowercase letters (a to f) contain only out-of-course false course-heads.

In Major, groups that include both in-course and out-of-course false course-heads always occur as complete groups. However, in Royal and above, in-course and out-of-course components of a group may occur independently. Therefore in the Methods Library, for Major methods, a single column contains the respective false course-head groups. However in Royal and higher, there are two columns headed "In" and "Out", which list the applicable false course-head groups. For example, the presence of letter E under "In" would indicate the false course-heads 32465 and 43265 for that method, whereas letter E listed under "Out" would indicate the false course-heads 25364 and 24635 for that method. If both of these pairs of false course-heads were present, E would be included under both "In" and "Out".

A further consideration in Royal and higher is that certain of the groups defined above, K, L, N, P, U and a, can subdivide. The subdivisions, indicated by K1, K2, L1, L2, N1, N2, P1, P2, U1, U2, and a1, a2 are also shown in the table above. A Royal or higher method might therefore include U1 in the "In" column rather than U, or N2 in the "Out" column rather than N.

4.

Methods without any in-course false course-heads are referred to as "cps", or clear proof scale. However, these methods will usually have out-of-course false course-heads. Yorkshire Surprise Major is an example of a cps method.


5.

A method that is false in the plain course will have false course-head group A, usually in addition to other groups.
