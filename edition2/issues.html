Suggestions for Future Versions of the Framework
1.
Consider separating out vanilla and exotic concepts into different parts of the framwork.
2.
Should more detail on handstroke gaps, cartwheeling, bell spacing, and other striking considerations be added to the Framework?
3.
Review method extension to see if there is scope for simplification and/or for making the extension processes more generalised so a greater number of cases are covered. Also consider whether extension processes might be made advisory, rather than fixed requirements.
4.
Instead of capitalising defined terms, considering italicising them, and/or making them hyperlinks. The latter would also allow the defined terms to be underlined and/or shown in a different color.
5.
Revisit the color scheme of the website -- this is limited in v1 by the Bootstrap color options. Includes looking at how to make hyperlinks stand out more.
6.
Ideally store the Framework's individual sections in a database so that future changes to sections can be tracked, versioned, and a history of changes can be automatically produced. Content type could also be stored in the database (e.g. whether the content is an example, a further explanation, etc) and then the presentation of the website could be driven off the type of the content.
7.
Consider whether to add a 'jump size' notation to the Framework:
jump-0 = identity changes;
jump-1 = adjacent changes;
jump-2 = no bell jumps more than two places;
jump-3 = no bell jumps more than three places;
etc.
8.
Revist 6.A.1 c). E.g. should PABS's 6 * 840 of an Alliance Minor method that produces six extents of Minor and six extents of Doubles be described in the performance title as 5040 Doubles and Minor? Or should the performance title be 5040 [Name] Alliance Minor and the performance detail covers the accepted truth feature?
Related to this, should 5040 of Martyrs Link Major rung with the 3rd unaffected throughout (using jumps calls) have a performance title of 5040 Triples, as is currently in 6.A.1 c), or should the title be 5040 Martyrs Link Major and the performance detail covers that the effective stage of the performance is Triples?
More broadly, should the performance title specify the effective stage of the performance (or the two effective stages in the case of accepted truth) such that a performance of Stedman Cinques and Bristol Max would be reported as (say) 5148 Maximus (where Maximus is the effective stage), and then the performance detail would say 2m: 2508 Stedman Cinques, 2640 Bristol Surprise Maximus.
Also, consider how side by side performances should be reported. E.g. should 'Simultaneous' be used in a similar way to Spliced and Variable Cover.
9.
Hunters with jump changes don't classify any further than the Hunter level in version 1 of the Framework. Consider adding a Treble Jump hunter class, and consider letting hunters with working bell jumps be Surprise, Delight, etc if the treble has the right path, and the other requirements for Surprise, Delight, etc are met. Open question on whether a TD method with jump changes can have jumps in the cross section changes.
10.
Version 1 of the Framework is difficult to read on a mobile device. Look into how this could be improved in future versions.
11.
Consider recognizing variations at stages other than Doubles. E.g. April Day Triples is quite commonly rung.
12.
Consider adding information on standard calls to the Framework -- e.g. 2nd place methods usally use 14 bobs and 1234 singles, nth's place methods usually use 1(n-2) bobs and 1(n-2)(n-1)(n) singles, etc, though exceptions for Bristol, Kent, etc. Should be coordinated with the implementation of the calls library.
13.
Stationary bells are currently classified as Treble Place. This means a method with an Alliance path and a stationary bell comes out as Treble Place, when Alliance might be more intuitive. Possible options are:
- Make stationary paths Hybrid (especially since we now know there are still hybrid methods (multi-hunt) that don't have asymmetric paths);
- Swap the order of precedence of Treble Place and Alliance;
- Create a separate hunt class for stationary paths.
